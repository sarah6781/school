Learn to Speak the Hindi Language Through Online Classes

Hindi is a widely spoken language in India. It is the mother language of more than 44% of the Indian population. Hindi is mostly spoken in the northern and central parts of India. When you go to the south of India, people mostly prefer speaking their mother tongues such as Telugu, Tamil, Marathi, etc. depending on the region they are residing in. Other than their regional language, a high percentage of the southern Indian population speak the English language. 

When you travel north, almost every household speak the Hindi language or some different dialect of it. India is a diverse nation, and across every 100 km, the language, clothes, and living style of people differ. 

Although, languages such as Punjabi, Himachali, Haryanvi have their own scripts they have a massive co-relation with the Hindi language. One who speaks Hindi can easily figure out what is being said in these regional languages because the grammar does not have a great distinction among them.  

Hindi is a part of India in many ways and learning Hindi can help you feel the real ethos of the Indian culture. Hindi as a language itself is very beautiful and expressive 

Why you should learn Hindi?

Learning Hindi will open up a lot of opportunities for you to explore. We have compiled a few ways in which Hindi can be a huge help to you.

  • New friends

One of the perks of learning any new language is being able to make new native friends and communicate with them. There are millions of people who speak and understand Hindi so knowing the language opens your doorway to new friendships and connections. It also happens to be the official language of India. 

  • Join the Club

There is a huge eclectic club of waiting for you after you become fluent in the language. Learning an additional language makes you bilingual and able to understand other dialects too. You will be able to see how diverse the language and people are around the country.

  • Work Opportunities

If you plan on moving to India for work you should make an effort to learn Hindi. India is a huge country with a vast number of industries and job opportunities. You will easily find a suitable job and learning Hindi will be an added benefit. There are a lot of outsourced jobs in India as well if you are an employer you will be able to converse with your employees in Hindi. This will help you in forming new connections that can flourish beyond just work. 

  • Binge on some amazing Bollywood Films

Learning Hindi will make you appreciate Bollywood movies even more without the translations and subtitles. Thousands of Hindi movies are released every year and they are packed with a tonne of entertainment. You will never run out of entertainment. There are several informative documentaries in Hindi too that you can binge on. 

  • Jam to Bollywood music

Update your playlist with peppy Hindi songs and groove to them. You will be able to jam at them better while you can understand them too. Enjoy both new and old classics of Hindi songs with your newly acquired Hindi skills. 

  • Experience more Spirituality

Hindi can make you more familiar with the rich culture of India. You will see how deeply spiritual the country is with so many religious activities and prayers surrounding Hinduism in Hindi. Also, you can read their spiritual texts, fictional books of stories and poems written in the Devanagari script. 

  • Rich Culture to explore 

India is rich in cultural antiques and portrays it very beautifully. With the knowledge of the language, you will see yourself have a better understanding of the culture. You will see everything from a new perspective and learn a thing or two from it to incorporate in your own life. 

  • Learn more

Learning a new language is like an initial step for you to learn even more on your way to reaching your maximum potential. You will have a boost of new confidence and zeal to learn even more. When you learn a new language your brain becomes more susceptible to learn even more with more developed cognitive skills.
